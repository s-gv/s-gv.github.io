Academic Research

Remote Neonatal Monitoring and Intervention
The Remote Neonatal Monitoring and Intervention Project is a collaborative effort with St. Johns Research Institute that aims to improve infant mortality in rural India by remotely monitoring physiological parameters of newborns and initiating timely action when the sensed parameters are amiss. The circuitry that senses physiological parameters has to consume very little power because the battery powering the system is quite small. So, I designed a low-power oxygen saturation sensor interface circuit and a wearable low-power electrocardiograph. I also developed a web application that collects sensor data and presents it to doctors. To aid building and debugging sensor interface circuits, I developed a streaming data visualization tool.
Minimizing energy consumption in sub-threshold digital circuits
When performance isn't a concern, energy consumption of digital circuits can be reduced by lowering the supply voltage. However, at very low supply voltages, so much time is spent on computation that the leakage energy starts to dominate. So, it would be counter-productive to decrease the supply voltage beyond this Minimum Energy Point (MEP). I built an energy sensing circuit that senses how much energy the digital load circuit is consuming at a given supply voltage. This information can be used to select the optimal supply voltage during circuit operation.

All-Digital NTSC Color generation
A technique to generate double sideband modulated color NTSC televesion signals using only a microcontroller and a shift register. It improves on Steve Wozniak’s Apple II design by lowering chroma-luma interference and adds compatibility with modern flat screen TVs.
